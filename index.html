Gautam Rituraj, Ph.D.

g.rituraj@alumni.iitg.ac.in, gautamrtrj91@gmail.com

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Technische Universiteit Delft (TU Delft), the Netherlands, in DC systems, Energy Conversion and Storage group, working on various national and international projects with industry partners.

Prior to joining TU Delft, I completed my PhD from the Electronics and Electrical Engineering (EEE) Department, IIT Guwahati, India, where I worked on different approaches to maximizing the transmission efficiency and power transfer capability of the wireless power transfer (WPT) system for EV charging applications.

My current research interests include developing integration software (using S2 standards) for hybrid energy storage systems, designing and analyzing of off-grid EV chargers (plug-in), standalone microgrid design and optimization, and digital twins for electrolyzer systems. It also includes analyzing and designing the wireless charging coils (magnetics) and developing the power converters in the WPT system for EV charging applications. Besides that, it consists of designing and developing a smart solar home system with multiple charging strategies for rural areas in India.

I am always excited to explore new ideas that can make a meaningful impact. I work with industry partners and researchers to create better energy solutions that contribute to a more sustainable future.

  • PhD Thesis: Coupling Efficiency Improvement and Power Transfer Enhancement in Wireless Power Transfer System for Electric Vehicle Charging Applications (key contributions are highlighted below :) [2013 - 2021]

    • Built the experimental setup of SP compensated WPT system with the battery as a load (used Si MOSFETs, high-frequency diodes, Litz wire (handmade), polypropylene capacitors, and FPGA board) and derived analytical expression of the optimum operating frequency to achieve the maximum transmission efficiency throughout the load variation in the charging process (CC and CV modes).
    • Developed a 3-D analytical model for the conventional rectangular (and square) air-core coils (of any dimensions) to calculate the magnetic field densities, self- and mutual inductance, and coupling (k).
    • Invented a novel unipolar coil arrangement method (UCAM) for improving k compared with conventional coils of the same self-inductance and outer dimensions (without flux-guiding materials) in WPT systems (also proposed its analytical model) [Indian patent no. 497323].
    • Proposed a new and simple ferrite arrangement of the rectangular (and square) coil structures (conventional and proposed) to minimize the ferrite volume while maintaining the improvement in k obtained from traditional ferrite arrangement.

    • Collaboratively developed and tested the hardware setup, fixed the bugs, and verified the proposed concept. Also contributed to its documentation.

  • Collaborative research project within the EEE Department: Design and analysis of wireless power transfer system for electric vehicle charging [2015 - 2019]
